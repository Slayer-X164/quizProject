What is Constitution?

A constitution is a foundational legal document that outlines the principles and framework of a government.

Basic knowledge of the Indian Constitution!

Length: It is one of the longest written constitutions in the world.
Fundamental Rights: It guarantees fundamental rights to all citizens, such as the right to equality, freedom of speech, and protection against discrimination.
Directive Principles: It includes Directive Principles of State Policy, which guide the government in policy-making for social and economic justice.
Federal Structure: India has a federal structure with a division of powers between the central government and states.
Parliamentary System: It establishes a parliamentary system of governance, with a President as the ceremonial head and a Prime Minister as the executive head.

Part V of the Indian Constitution deals with the Union and includes Articles 52 to 151. It outlines the framework for the central government:

Articles 52-78: Structure and powers of the President, Vice President, and the Council of Ministers.
Articles 79-122: Formation and functioning of Parliament (Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha), including legislative processes.
Articles 123-151: Powers of the President regarding legislation and the procedures for the establishment of various authorities.

Part VI includes Articles 152 to 237

Articles 152-166: Framework for the Governor, state legislatures, and their powers.
Articles 167-190: Legislative procedures and the powers of the state assemblies.
Articles 191-237: Election procedures, the distribution of powers between the states and the Union, and provisions for the administration of union territories.
